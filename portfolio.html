Tennis ELO What-If Calculator

January 2024

I created this project over my winter break to begin a series of statistics projects designed to flesh out my portfolio. I really love tennis and its history, which is why I decided to create a dashboard to graph the ELO rankings of all tennis players over time (ELO is a metric used to assess performance). I also added the feature to omit certain players from history, as tennis fans often discuss what tennis history would be like if, say, Federer did not have Nadal to stop him on clay. I am really proud of this first statistics project.

Gamification in the classroom: How different types of leaderboards and levels of goal-setting affect performance and motivation in a problem solving task

September 2020 - May 2021

I completed this rather ambitious project back in high school. To summarize, I created an online multiplayer slide puzzle game to test if different types of leaderboards and goals (rewards for top 1, 3, 5, etc.) affected performance in a cognitive task. I used Pygame to make the game, and a Google Sheets API to store the data during and after students in various math classes played the game. Many students experienced the game crashing (likely due to a read-write quota in the free version). Looking back, I also doubt that I got enough data to achieve statistically significant results. Yet, I think that the end product was really cool, and was my first time engineering an entire data pipeline.
